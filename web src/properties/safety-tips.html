Personal Safety

  • Stay in well-lit, populated areas, especially at night.
  • Keep your phone charged and accessible for emergencies.
  • Remain aware of your surroundings—limit distractions like loud music.
  • Trust your instincts—if something feels off, leave or seek help immediately.

Home Security

  • Install secure locks on doors and windows.
  • Use outdoor motion-sensor lights to deter intruders.
  • Keep valuables out of sight—close blinds or curtains at night.
  • Consider installing a home security system or camera.

Video Tutorial: Home Security Tips

Online Safety is just as important as Personal Safety

  • Use strong, unique passwords for each account.
  • Enable two-factor authentication wherever possible.
  • Stay cautious of suspicious links or phishing emails.
  • Keep your software and antivirus up to date.
  • Don't send strangers you met online any sensistive information.
  • Avoid installing random apps as these can contain malicious code to steal your information that can be used to blackmail you!

Community Awareness

  • Join or start a local Neighborhood Watch group.
  • Report suspicious activities to the authorities promptly.
  • Stay informed on local crime trends and alerts.
  • Encourage neighbors to look out for one another.
